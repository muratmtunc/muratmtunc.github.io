Secrets to Survival of Blockchain Projects

Murat M. Tunc, Eric Zheng
September 2019

Abstract

Why do most Blockchain projects fail? Although hundred thousands of blockhain projects and applications have emerged, the essence of their sustainability in the long run and the secrets to their survival remain largely unexplored. In this paper, we take a first step to empirically investigate the key issues to blockchain projects’ longevity and survival by analyzing the behavior of the users of a Blockchain platform. We find evidence that users who produce tokens are more long-lasting than resellers, with the implication that the blockchain projects should reward the users who produce tokens more. On the other hand, resellers are also beneficial to the marketplace, since their presence increase the price of a token. However, they tend to churn quicker, which may expedite the collapse of a blockchain project. In order to achieve longevity and sustainability, blockchain projects need to carefully consider the double-sword nature of resellers when designing its incentive mechanism.
